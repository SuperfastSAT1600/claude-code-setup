# GEO Content Quality & E-E-A-T Assessment Framework

This document outlines a comprehensive methodology for evaluating content quality through the lens of E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) and AI citability.

## Core Framework

The assessment divides evaluation across four equal dimensions, each worth 25 points:

**Experience** focuses on first-hand knowledge, with scoring for personal accounts, original research, case studies with results, authentic evidence, specific examples, and process demonstrations.

**Expertise** examines professional competence through author credentials, technical depth, methodology clarity, data-backed claims, correct terminology usage, and detailed author backgrounds.

**Authoritativeness** measures external validation via inbound citations, media mentions, industry awards, speaking credentials, peer-reviewed publications, topical coverage, and encyclopedic references.

**Trustworthiness** assesses transparency through contact information, privacy policies, HTTPS security, editorial standards, business model clarity, customer reviews, claim accuracy, and sponsorship disclosures.

## Key Assessment Areas

The framework establishes content quality metrics including word count benchmarks (ranging from 300-5,000+ words depending on page type), readability targets (Flesch Reading Ease of 60-70), and optimal paragraph structures of 2-4 sentences leading with key claims.

It distinguishes between acceptable AI-generated content and problematic patterns like generic phrasing, lack of original insight, and hedging overload. High-quality signals include original data, specific examples, contrarian views backed by reasoning, and trade-offs acknowledged.

Topical authority assessment rewards sites with 20+ comprehensive pages as "Authority" level, while thin coverage under 5 pages incurs penalties.

Final scores range from 0-100, with 85-100 indicating exceptional citation candidates and 0-39 requiring fundamental strategy overhauls.
